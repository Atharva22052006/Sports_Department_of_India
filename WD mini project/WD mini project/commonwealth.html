Common Wealth Games 2018


The 2018 Commonwealth Games in Gold Coast, Australia, were a historic moment for Indian sports. India's exceptional performance secured them the third position on the medal table, a testament to the nation's growing sporting prowess.

Shooting Dominance:

The Indian shooting team emerged as a force, clinching 16 medals, including 7 gold. Young sensation Manu Bhaker, at just 16 years old, set a new Games record in the women's 10m air pistol event, showcasing India's bright future in shooting.



Apart from Manu Bhaker's record-breaking performance, other notable achievements in shooting included gold medals by Heena Sidhu in the women's 10m air pistol and Jitu Rai in the men's 50m pistol.

Badminton Brilliance:

P.V. Sindhu led the Indian badminton contingent to 2 gold and 2 bronze medals, further elevating the sport's popularity in the nation.



P.V. Sindhu's gold medal in women's singles was complemented by Lakshya Sen's gold in men's singles and the men's doubles pair of Satwiksairaj Rankireddy and Chirag Shetty's gold medal win.

Table Tennis Triumph:

Manika Batra shone brightly in table tennis, contributing significantly to India's medal tally with 2 gold and 4 bronze medals.



Manika Batra's gold medals in women's singles and mixed doubles, along with Sharath Kamal's gold in men's singles, highlighted India's growing strength in table tennis.

Weightlifting Prowess:

India reigned supreme in weightlifting, securing 3 gold, 5 silver, and 2 bronze medals. This stellar performance solidified India's position as a weightlifting powerhouse.Apart from that, Mirabai Chanu, a renowned weightlifter, added another gold to her illustrious career in the women's 49kg category.

Wrestling Triumphs:

Indian wrestlers displayed remarkable strength, winning 2 gold, 2 silver, and 2 bronze medals. Also, Bajrang Punia, Sakshi Malik, and Deepak Punia secured gold medals in their respective weight categories, showcasing India's dominance in wrestling.

A New Era for Indian Sports:

The 2018 Commonwealth Games marked a turning point for Indian sports. The impressive medal haul not only boosted national pride but also inspired a new generation of athletes. India's consistent performance across various disciplines demonstrated the nation's growing sporting potential and its journey towards becoming a global sporting powerhouse.
