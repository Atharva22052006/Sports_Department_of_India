Frequently Asked Questions

Your queries about the Department of Sports are answered here!

The Department of Sports promotes and develops sports nationwide through policy-making, infrastructure development, and athlete support. It provides financial aid, scholarships, and training, organizes national and international events, and fosters youth participation. Additionally, it works to ensure inclusivity in sports, supporting women, differently-abled individuals, and marginalized communities.

Athletes can apply for government support through the official Department of Sports portal by accessing programs like scholarships, training grants, and financial aid schemes. They need to register, provide necessary documentation, and meet eligibility criteria specific to the program.

Yes, scholarships are available for young athletes through various government programs. These scholarships support training, education, and participation in competitions. Details and application guidelines can be found on the Department of Sports website.

You can access training facilities under the Department of Sports by applying through their official website or directly contacting registered sports academies and centers of excellence. Eligibility criteria and documentation may vary based on the facility and program.

Yes, the Department of Sports provides funding for organizing sports events through specific government schemes. Event organizers can apply for financial support by submitting proposals and meeting the eligibility criteria outlined by the department.
